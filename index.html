Abstract

Head swapping is a task that generates seamless results by replacing the human head of the target image with the head of the source image, which can be used in various real-world applications. In this paper, we point out that the existing head swapping framework shows artifacts on the background and foreground (body and neck) regions and the unclear borders between them, especially in challenging and practical cases where a large head shape and hair differences exist between the source and the target. To overcome this problem, we propose Chroma-HS, a framework that splits the head swapping task into the background generation and foreground generation. Chroma-HS proposes a new scheme by incorporating the chroma key technique in the background for the first time, which enables a diverse flawless background generation. In addition, Chroma-HS uses Foreground-Prediction and -Aware Transformer (FPAT) to generate the foreground without the artifact from the background by restricting the attention of transformer blocks with the predicted foreground region. Finally, Chroma-HS mitigates the challenging case by training the model with our novel Head shape and long Hair augmentation (H2 augmentation). Experimental results show that our Chroma-HS significantly outperforms the state-of-the-art head swapping model on benchmark datasets both qualitatively and quantitatively.

Motivations

We propose Chroma-HS to consider the real-world application. As shown in (a), the existing work [2] (HeSer) shows severe artifacts on inpainting regions. To inpaint the background flawlessly, we propose to introduce chroma keying in the head swapping framework. However, it still shows low-fidelity results to inpaint the neck and the body, which is hidden due to the head shape and hair difference described in a red box of (b). Chroma-HS generates the high-fidelity foreground with Foreground-Prediction and -Aware Transformer (FPAT) and H2 augmentation. Chroma-HS removes artifacts as shown in the blue boxes of (b) and (c), and easily changes various high-fidelity real-world backgrounds.

Methods

Chroma-HS consists of H2 augmentation, an encoder (E), a head colorizer, a body blender including Foreground-Prediction and -Aware Transformer (FPAT) modules, which are denoted with the red box, and a decoder (D). We visualize our proposed input (X) manipulation method (the blue box) with overall training and inference schemes. The head colorizer colorizes the gray head of X, and the body blender inpaints the hidden body and the neck with a foreground mask-aware attention mechanism.
