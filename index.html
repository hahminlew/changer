Abstract

Head swapping task aims at seamlessly transplanting a source head onto a target body. Yet, the existing head swapping methods show imperfect results in inpainting the background region, which leads to a reliability issue for the industrial application systems. Therefore, we propose to transfer the head swapping task to a chroma key setting to generate flawless results for industrial applications. However, we observe that simply adopting the previous head swapping to the green screen scenario still shows low-fidelity results due to (a) the unnatural generation of a gap between the source head and the target body and (b) flawed colorization of the swapped head.

In this paper, we propose Chroma-HS, a new Head Swapping framework in Chroma key setting, which achieves high-fidelity head swapping results by focusing on the neck generation and conditional colorization task while utilizing chroma keying on the background. In Chroma-HS, we introduce a novel input manipulation that simulates the mismatched neck regions in the swapping procedure, resulting in a robust neck generation on a green screen scenario. In addition, we introduce a new Conditional Colorization Module (CoCoMo) based on a cross-attention mechanism to improve the fidelity of the swapped head. Experimental results show that our Chroma-HS significantly outperforms the state-of-the-art head swapping model on benchmark datasets qualitatively and quantitatively. Moreover, we demonstrate the effectiveness of our model using extensive in-depth studies. The source code will be available at our github.
